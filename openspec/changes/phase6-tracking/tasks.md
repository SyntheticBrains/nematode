> **T7 two-regimes checkpoint (2026-06-14, mid-T7-prep / Stage-1 substrate canary).** The Stage-1 canary (predator + thermotaxis bring-up on the calibrated continuous substrate) forced a reframing of the **T4↔T7 comparison**. The continuous substrate is **not commensurable** with the T4 grid at the parameter level: float `(speed, turn)` movement with fine radian control, Euclidean-disc vs Manhattan-diamond detection/damage geometry, optimal-bearing vs cardinal predator pursuit, Fick fields, adaptive sensing, and per-step metabolic timescales all shift together, so a carried-over number (`detection_radius`, `damage_radius`, `predator.speed`, satiety) does **not** mean the same difficulty across substrates (the `damage_radius = 0` bug — valid on grid, unreachable on continuous — is the proof case). **Consequences:** **(1)** the **within-T7 architecture ranking** (Gate 3 G3.a — all 6 MUST arms on the *same* continuous substrate) is **unaffected and remains the clean primary result**; **(2)** the **T4→T7 env-delta (RQ5 / G3.c)** was never a controlled single-variable delta and is **downgraded to a qualitative cross-regime comparison** ("do the same architectures learn the same repertoire on a much higher-fidelity world, and does the top cluster's character survive the jump?") with non-commensurability documented as a limitation; **(3)** the continuous substrate is calibrated **on its own terms** — biological fidelity where it applies (chemotaxis gradient/diffusion lengths, thermotaxis assay gradients) + a **multi-objective learnable band** (trained-best clearly above an untrained/random floor and below saturation) — **not** by matching T4 numbers. The **reward formula is upgraded once to continuous-native coherence** — Euclidean distances replacing the residual Manhattan predator-distance the reward still computes (`T7.prep.reward_coherence`) — then **frozen across all T7 arms** (coherence-only; no per-arch or shaping tuning — that single consistent reward is what keeps the within-T7 ranking clean, NOT matching T4's formula). *Substrate* difficulty (predator kinematics, thermal gradient) is then calibrated against that coherent reward, exactly as T4 itself dropped combined-cell `predator_damage` 20→6 for stacked learnability. **Primary aim:** demonstrate the high-fidelity continuous substrate supports **multi-objective learning**, anchored externally by **real-worm behavioural-chemotaxis validation** (`T7.validation`), which gives the chemotaxis fidelity its meaning in place of T4. *Affected T7/Gate-3/RQ5 rows carry inline `(amended 2026-06-14)` notes. A controlled **fidelity-ablation** — a deliberately "grid-like" continuous config to decompose which fidelity change moves the ranking — is recorded as a deferred optional stretch (the only path that could recover a controlled decomposition; out of Phase-6 scope now).*

> **T7 C1/C2 convergence checkpoint (2026-06-14, post-Stage-1).** Stage 1 confirmed the substrate is *learnable* but surfaced that **nothing has converged to genuinely *good* learning** on the high-fidelity continuous substrate — foraging "success" is the ≥0.5-foods/episode marker (~1 of a 10 target, ~10%), vs the grid's 73–84% *task* success at T4. Building the C3 architecture ranking on marginal-everywhere foraging would rank noise, and would hollow out the "high-fidelity substrate supports multi-objective learning" claim if single-objective foraging itself barely works. We have so far calibrated **substrate difficulty** but not **architecture optimisation** or **genuine convergence** (the two are distinct). **Consequence — C1/C2 are promoted from n=1 de-risk smokes to per-architecture convergence + optimisation evaluations, run before C3:** **(1)** C1 (foraging-only) and C2 (foraging+predator) run **per MUST arch, n≥4, to convergence**, with **time-boxed per-arch tuning** allowed (entropy/lr + arch-specific levers — window / units / layers) to bring each arch to its achievable ceiling — front-loading the architecture optimisation T4 did *reactively* inside its C3 cell; **(2)** that per-arch tuning is an **explicit, fair part of the comparison** (compare architectures at their best, not as-happened-to-be-tuned), recorded, with the C1/C2 ceilings then *interpreting* the C3 per-behaviour sub-metrics (a low C3 predator-survival reflects the arch's C2 ceiling, not the integration); **(3)** **substrate-revisit is on the table** — if, after honest per-arch tuning, *no* arch converges to good foraging, that is evidence the foraging substrate is **too hard** (the Fick flat-top — `T7.prep.foraging_calibration` only partly mitigated it with capture 2.0), and the foraging difficulty (flat-top mitigation / capture / world scale) MAY be revisited to make genuine convergence achievable while staying as high-fidelity as convergence permits; so **`T7.prep.foraging_calibration`'s substrate is provisional pending C1 convergence**. **Discrimination expectation:** C1 (foraging) is **not** expected to separate architectures (T4 found architecture does not discriminate on foraging — the top cluster tied); its job is to confirm each arch *can* genuinely forage so C3 isn't built on sand. Architecture differences emerge at **C2 (predator)** — already previewed by the Stage-1 finding that the Transformer does not learn reactive continuous predator-evasion. *Affected curriculum-smoke + Stage 3/4 rows carry inline `(amended 2026-06-14, C1/C2 convergence)` notes.*

> **T7 new-architecture-candidate research checkpoint (2026-06-29, post-029).** First new-architecture scan since T4. NEAT especially unpromising given GA's 029 last-place). **Correction:** the earlier "`normalize_advantages` as a re-rank foundation" idea is **dropped** — the recurrent collapse it was meant to treat was the `fix-continuous-distance-reward-metric` reward bug (since fixed), and the explored PopArt value-normalization change was already dropped as not load-bearing; the memory gate is the single real prerequisite. *Affected: `T7.separation.*` gains a gated `new_arch_candidates` task + a non-gated `ncp_fidelity_arm` task; `T7.prep.should_may_continuous` gains the research-verdict note.*
